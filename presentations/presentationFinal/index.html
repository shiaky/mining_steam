Get in touch with our storyline

Initial idea

Predict genre/owned games of a player based on information given through friend profiles.

Didn't work out because data wasn't as rich as estimated.

So we made a pivot

There are several types of players.

In our case, we divide them into two groups:

Casual players and Non-casual players

Why not take all information we can get from the players friends and infer if the player is a casual or non-casual player?

But wait!

How to decide if a player is casual or non-casual?

First of all, there is no uniform definition of a casual player.

For us, players of this category don't play as much as other players. They have varying online times through the week and don't plan to play in comparison to non-casual players.

Based on an American Time Use Survey Summary* people spend a bit more than 5 hours on leisure activities per day [1]:

On an average day, nearly everyone age 15 and over (96 percent) engaged in some sort of leisure activity, such as watching TV, socializing, or exercising. Men spent 44 minutes per day more in these activities than did women (5.5 hours, compared with 4.8 hours).

So we classify users that have a playtime less than or equal to 1 hour as casual and greater than 1 hour per day as non-casual

[1] https://www.bls.gov/news.release/atus.nr0.htm

A problem that occurred was the unprecise playtime data of Steam. For each player the Steam API only provided data of the last 14 days and the total playtime. Because there are no daily statistics, we used the total playtime in relation to the registration date of the player.
